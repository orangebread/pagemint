My read

  • Red — Strongest signal. Reads as a printer's registration mark, gives the brand a pulse. Risk: feels Swiss/editorial, not "minty."
  • Mint · deep (#4A7A5A) — Most cohesive with the name. Reads as a tasteful brand color, not a candy. If you go mint, this is the one. The deep tone holds against cream without disappearing and doesn't feel like a 2010s startup.
  • Mint · mid (#6B9377) — Pleasant but quiet. The dot loses presence at 48px and the brand loses its accent. Skip.
  • Mint · bright (#5BB37A) — Drifts into Spotify/Robinhood territory. Saturated mint = exactly the AI palette we just escaped. Skip.

Recommendation: If brand cohesion matters more (it usually does for a small product trying to differentiate), Mint · deep. If pulse matters more (a single warm accent that says "this is alive"), keep Red. There's no wrong answer — both clear the AI-slop bar. Pick the one that matches how you want PageMint to feel: confident-quiet (mint) or confident-warm (red).
